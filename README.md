Vendor Risk Management Project
Overview

This project simulates a Third-Party Risk Management (TPRM) assessment conducted for a fintech company evaluating a new vendor. The goal of this project is to demonstrate the vendor risk management lifecycle, including vendor risk identification, due diligence, control analysis, and remediation planning.

The assessment evaluates CloudDocs Inc., a cloud-based document storage provider, which will store sensitive financial documentation and personally identifiable information (PII) for SentinelPay.

Because the vendor processes sensitive customer data, a structured vendor risk assessment was conducted to evaluate security, compliance, and operational risks.

Company Scenario

Company: SentinelPay
Industry: Financial Technology (Fintech)
Program: Third-Party Risk Management (TPRM)

SentinelPay relies on third-party vendors to support core business operations. Vendors that access sensitive data must undergo a formal risk assessment before onboarding to ensure they maintain appropriate security and compliance controls.

Vendor Overview

Vendor: CloudDocs Inc.
Service: Cloud-based document storage and management platform

CloudDocs Inc. provides a cloud platform used for storing, managing, and processing digital documents. SentinelPay plans to use the service to store customer documentation including:

Loan agreements

Identity verification documents

Financial records

Because these documents contain sensitive information, the vendor introduces potential risks related to:

Information security

Privacy protection

Regulatory compliance

Operational availability

Reputational risk

For this reason, CloudDocs Inc. was classified as a High-Risk Vendor, requiring enhanced due diligence.

Vendor Risk Management Lifecycle

This project demonstrates a typical Third-Party Risk Management workflow.

Phase	Description
Vendor Identification	Business unit proposes vendor for onboarding
Risk Assessment	Vendor risk level determined based on service and data exposure
Due Diligence	Vendor documentation and security evidence reviewed
Control Evaluation	SOC 2 report and vendor security controls analyzed
Risk Documentation	Risks documented within a risk register
Remediation Planning	Control gaps addressed through remediation plan
Ongoing Monitoring	Vendor risk monitored throughout the relationship
Risk Assessment Summary
Risk Category	Rating
Inherent Risk	High
Control Effectiveness	Moderate
Residual Risk	Medium

CloudDocs Inc. maintains several strong controls including encryption, multi-factor authentication, and role-based access control. However, certain control weaknesses were identified that require remediation.

Key Risk Findings

The vendor risk assessment identified several potential risks:

Unauthorized access to customer PII due to delayed access reviews

Limited log retention that may reduce the ability to investigate security incidents

Operational disruption risk if vendor services become unavailable

While these risks do not prevent vendor onboarding, they require monitoring and remediation to reduce overall exposure.

Risk Scoring Methodology

Vendor risk was evaluated using a scoring model based on several risk factors.

Risk Factor	Description
Data Sensitivity	Type of data the vendor accesses or stores
System Access	Level of access to internal systems
Regulatory Impact	Potential compliance or legal exposure
Business Dependency	Importance of vendor services to operations
Fourth-Party Risk	Vendor reliance on subcontractors or cloud providers

Each factor is scored on a 1-5 scale, allowing the organization to determine a vendor’s overall risk score and risk tier.
